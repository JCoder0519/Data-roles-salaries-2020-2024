Exploring the Potential Determinants and Trend of Salary in Data-Related Roles from 2020-2024

Introduction

The field of data-related roles has experienced significant growth and evolution over the past few years, with a surge in demand for professionals skilled in data analysis, data science, machine learning, and other related domains. As organizations across various industries continue to recognize the value of data-driven decision-making, the demand for skilled data professionals has led to increased competition and scrutiny regarding salaries and compensation packages within this sector. In this project, I aim to explore the potential determinants and trends of salaries in data-related roles from 2020 to 2024. Specifically, I will address four key tasks:

  • Task 1: Understanding Data Jobs Salary Trend from 2020-2024

    Analyze the salary trends in data-related roles over the past five years to identify any notable patterns, fluctuations, or overall growth in compensation within the industry. By examining salary data from 2020 to 2024, we seek to gain insights into the trajectory of salaries for data professionals during this period.

  • Task 2: Exploring the Impact of Employment Type on Salary

    Investigate the impact of employment type (such as full-time, part-time, contract, or freelance) on salary levels within the data-related sector. By comparing salaries across different employment types, we aim to discern whether certain types of employment arrangements are associated with higher or lower compensation for data professionals.

  • Task 3: Exploring the Impact of Experience Level & Company Size on Salary

    Examine how experience level and company size influence salary levels within the data-related industry. By analyzing salary data based on experience levels (such as entry-level, mid-level, senior-level) and company sizes (small, medium, large), we aim to identify any correlations or trends between these factors and salary levels.

  • Task 4: Exploring the Median Salary of the Top 20 Most Common Jobs

    Focus on the top 20 most common job titles within the data-related sector and explore the median salary for each of these positions. By examining salary data for the most prevalent job titles, we aim to provide insights into the typical compensation levels for various roles within the data industry.

    • Task 5: Explore Salary Information by Country

    Investigate salary information by country to understand how compensation varies geographically in the data-related roles.

Through these analyses, we aim to provide valuable insights into the salary dynamics within the data-related sector, helping professionals, employers, and policymakers better understand the factors influencing compensation trends and make informed decisions regarding hiring, compensation packages, and career development strategies within this rapidly evolving field.

How to interact with this visualization?

  • Single Experience Level Selection: Users can click on any one of the experience levels—Entry, Mid, Senior, or Executive—in the legend to isolate and view the median salary distribution for only that category across the top 20 job titles.
  • Multiple Experience Levels Comparison: Users have the option to select and view multiple experience levels simultaneously. This comparison allows users to observe and compare the median salaries across different experience levels for each job title.
  • Zoom Functionality: Users can utilize the zoom feature to take a closer or broader look at the salary data presented for the various job titles, enhancing the detail or overview as required.
  • Hovering Data Details: Users can view the data in detail by placing the cursor over a data point, which will display specific salary information for that job title and experience level.

Task 5: Explore Salary Information by Country

Interactive Visualization Title

  • Hover Over Countries: Place the cursor over a country to display a tooltip with detailed descriptive statistics of jobs in that area. The tooltip includes the median salary, mean salary, standard deviation (std), minimum (min) salary, 25th percentile (25%), 75th percentile (75%), maximum (max) salary, and the count of data points (count) for the country.
  • Color Gradient Legend: Use the color gradient legend on the right to understand the median salary range across different countries. Countries with darker shades represent higher median salaries.
  • Zoom Functionality: Utilize the zoom controls to focus on a specific region or to get a broader view of global salary distributions.

Summary of Findings

Salary Trends by Company Size

Medium-sized companies are showing a substantial upward trajectory in salaries, surpassing both small and large companies. Large companies maintain higher salaries than small companies but are being overtaken by medium ones. Small companies have the least variation and the lowest salaries.

Salary by Experience Level

Salaries increase with experience across job titles, with executive roles having the highest median and mean salaries. There is a relatively symmetrical distribution of salaries for mid and senior levels, suggesting a standard progression in earnings as experience increases.

Salary Distribution by Employment Type

Full-time roles offer the most significant potential for high salaries and exhibit the broadest range, indicating varied compensation across different industries and job functions. Contract work provides moderately high salaries with less variability. Freelance and part-time roles generally have lower and more consistent salary distributions.

Salary Distribution by Job Title and Experience Level

The visualization demonstrates that, for the 20 most common data roles, the median salary varies by job title and experience level. Notable findings include the highest median salaries for entry-level applied scientists, mid-level machine learning scientists, senior ML engineers, and executive business intelligence engineers.

Global Salary Statistics

The interactive world map with descriptive statistics indicates a disparity in salaries based on geographical location. The United States appears to be the country with the highest median and mean salary for data roles in the world, showcasing significant global variations.

Findings and Learnings

Findings:

  • Medium-sized companies may be becoming competitive in their salary offerings.
  • Experience level is a strong indicator of salary potential, especially for executive positions.
  • Full-time employment typically offers better salary prospects compared to other types of employment.
  • There is a significant geographical influence on salary distribution.

Learnings:

  • Interactive elements such as zooming and tooltips enhance data comprehension.
  • Descriptive statistics are crucial for understanding the spread and central tendency of salaries across different segments.
  • Visual representations like color gradients can effectively convey complex information, such as median salary ranges across countries.

Future Directions

Further research could focus on investigating factors contributing to salary trends, exploring variances in full-time salaries, analyzing the impact of location on salary, and developing nuanced visualizations accounting for demographic impacts on salary.
